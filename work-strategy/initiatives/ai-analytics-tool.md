# Agentic AI Analytics Tool

## What It Is
A mature platform (owned by Data Engineering) that enables business stakeholders to interact with their data through agent-based experiences rather than static dashboards.

## Primary Users
- Primary: business stakeholders
- Secondary: analysts

## Core Jobs
- Stakeholders: "talk" to their data and explore questions beyond static dashboards
- Analysts: support theories, accelerate exploration, and access underlying SQL

## Team Role
Adoption enablement layer:
- Shepherd new use-case buildouts outside home vertical
- Host org-wide office hours
- Act as primary partner to platform team
- Beta test new features
- Drive feature adoption

## Current State
- Platform is mature
- Multiple agents already in production
- Built by analytics, platform team, and others

## Current Friction
- Analyst hesitancy to adopt new tooling
- Lack of critical mass of proficient users
- Default behavior still favors static dashboards

## Pilot Context (Recapture & Retention)
High-stakes pilot agent:
- Goal: enable senior stakeholders to self-serve analytics
- Replace granular ad hoc requests to analytics teams

### Success Criteria
- Outputs align with established reporting truth
- Supports executive "train of thought" exploration

### Risk Profile
- High visibility to senior stakeholders
- First major win for new senior analytics leader

## Ownership Reality
- Team will support initially
- Long-term owner not yet defined
- Receiving teams are not ready and not proficient with the tool

## Enablement State
- Platform team strong on ad hoc support
- Weak on patterns, documentation, reusable assets
- No repeatable adoption system yet

## Success (3–6 Months)
- Successful pilot launch
- Acceptable output accuracy
- Positive executive experience

## Explicit Non-Goals / Boundaries
- Team does not own platform
- Team does not own all agents
- Team should not own agents long-term
- Pilot is not proof of universal success
- Office hours alone will not solve adoption
