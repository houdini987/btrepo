# Centralized LLM Portal

## What It Is
An enterprise-wide platform exposing multiple LLMs and enabling users to create custom apps using prompts, files, and configurations.

## Primary Users
- Everyone in the company

## Core Capability
- Build individualized AI-powered apps
- Use prompts, file attachments, and sharing
- Enable self-service AI experimentation and solutions

## Team Role
- Current: consumer/user
- Target: power users and mentors

## Current State
- Broad adoption across company
- Highly flexible usage patterns

## Proven Use Case
- Helper app for thousands of users
- Supports understanding of complex reporting suite
- Low maintenance after launch

## Current Friction
- UI flakiness
- Inconsistent model ingestion of attachments
- Weak governance
- Poor discoverability

## Important Boundary
- Team does not own platform governance
- Team not responsible for enterprise adoption
- Platform team receives sufficient feedback from user base

## Success (3–6 Months)
- All team members have tried building an app
- General usability knowledge across team
- No requirement for production apps

## Explicit Non-Goals / Boundaries
- No ownership of governance
- No accountability for enterprise adoption
- No requirement for production apps
- No responsibility for reliability
